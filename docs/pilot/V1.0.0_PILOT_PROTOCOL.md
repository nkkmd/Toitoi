# Toitoi v1.0.0 Pilot Protocol

**Status: ready for participant scheduling** | **Version: 1.0** | **Last updated: 2026-07-22**

## Purpose

This protocol evaluates whether the Toitoi v1.0.0 reference implementation helps people turn field observations into reviewable inquiries, discover related inquiries without losing local context, and understand provenance, AI involvement, and lineage.

The pilot does not evaluate agronomic truth, certify legal compliance, or authorize autonomous AI publication.

## Recommended participant group

- growers or farmers: 2–3
- researcher: 1
- extension or technical practitioner: 1
- developer or operator: 1–2

A participant may hold more than one role in a small pilot, but role overlap must be recorded.

## Safety and information boundary

Before participation, explain and obtain acknowledgement that:

- raw observations may contain location, personal, confidential, traditional, or community knowledge
- raw observations remain separately controlled from published Canonical Events
- synchronization and publication are explicit actions
- AI output is a proposal and may be edited or rejected
- annotation acceptance does not equal publication approval
- participants should use the supplied reference field and fictional identifiers unless a separate real-data agreement exists
- screenshots and logs must be reviewed before external sharing
- correction, withdrawal, and deletion requests are handled through auditable state transitions

Do not collect names, precise coordinates, contact details, or commercially sensitive information unless they are necessary, consented to, and covered by an explicit handling plan.

## Reference scenario

Use the repository fixture in:

`fixtures/reference/v1.0.0/east-side-weed-scenario.json`

Scenario prompt:

> 畑の東側だけ雑草の種類が違う。東側にはスゲに似た草が多く、西側には少ない。

The participant should be able to complete or inspect the following sequence:

1. record the observation while offline
2. confirm local persistence
3. review detected sensitive information
4. reconnect and explicitly synchronize
5. inspect AI-generated inquiry candidates
6. edit, accept, or reject a candidate
7. promote the accepted candidate to an Inquiry Draft
8. submit the Draft for independent publication review
9. inspect the published Canonical Event and transport delivery states
10. search for the related inquiry from another region
11. inspect local vocabulary and shared-concept mapping
12. create or inspect a `synthesizes` derivation
13. inspect lineage, provenance, AI involvement, and human decisions
14. inspect replay or recovery evidence presented by the operator

## Moderator script

### Opening

Explain that the aim is to observe the workflow, not to test the participant. Ask the participant to describe what they expect before each major action.

### During tasks

Use neutral prompts:

- What do you think will happen when you select this action?
- What information do you believe will leave the device?
- Who do you think has approved this content?
- What does this status mean to you?
- What would you do next in your normal work?

Do not teach the correct answer until the participant has completed or abandoned the task.

### Closing

Ask:

- Did the inquiry candidate broaden or narrow your thinking?
- Was important local context preserved?
- Was the distinction between AI review and publication approval clear?
- Was the related inquiry from another region useful?
- Could you identify the source observation and subsequent derivations?
- Were any status labels confusing?
- What information would you avoid entering?
- Would you use this workflow again, and for what kind of observation?

## Observation record

For each task record:

- participant role
- session mode: in-person, remote, self-guided
- device and viewport class
- task started and completed
- completion without assistance: yes/no
- assistance required
- elapsed time
- errors or abandoned actions
- participant interpretation
- privacy or safety concern
- issue severity: blocker, major, moderate, minor, observation
- suggested follow-up

Use anonymous participant identifiers such as `P01-grower`.

## Success signals

The pilot provides useful evidence when participants can:

- save an observation offline without assuming it is published
- identify and confirm sensitive information before synchronization
- distinguish AI-generated text from human-approved publication
- find a related inquiry while recognizing that it is not the same Canonical Event
- understand at least the main direction of lineage
- identify provenance and AI involvement
- preserve or recognize local terminology
- explain how to correct, withdraw, or revise published material

These signals are observational. They are not release claims until recorded in the findings document.

## Stop conditions

Stop or pause a session when:

- a participant enters unapproved personal, precise-location, confidential, or community-restricted information
- the participant believes AI output is authoritative and the misunderstanding cannot be corrected safely
- publication scope is unclear
- a security, data-loss, or irreversible-publication defect is observed
- the participant withdraws consent

Record the condition without retaining unnecessary sensitive content.

## Evidence handling

- store task records separately from published Canonical Events
- anonymize participant identifiers in repository findings
- do not commit raw recordings, contact details, exact locations, or unreviewed screenshots
- report observed facts separately from moderator interpretation
- disclose missing roles, shortened sessions, simulated steps, and other limitations

## Completion gate

The pilot is complete only when:

- participant composition and role overlap are recorded
- all completed and skipped tasks are recorded
- safety observations are triaged
- findings are anonymized
- known limitations and follow-up decisions are published in `V1.0.0_PILOT_FINDINGS.md`

Until then, the repository must describe the pilot as planned, scheduled, partial, or not conducted—not completed.
